After 10+ hours of testing, here are my top 10 hyper-optimized Perplexity Computer prompts — plus an honest breakdown of how it stacks against OpenClaw and Manus, and how to use all three together.

The 10 Mega Prompts

10 Finance

Ultimate Financial Research

Automated institutional-grade financial research using Perplexity's excellent financial data-sourcing. Full 12-step research cycle: mandate, business model, historical financials, ratio analysis, capital allocation, competitive positioning, macro drivers, market expectations, valuation, risk red-team, variant perception, and investment memo.

View full prompt ↓
Act as my institutional-grade financial research analyst with experience in equity, macro, and crypto markets. Your job is to perform a full research cycle on [asset / ticker / token / sector / theme] over [time horizon] for an investor with [risk profile + style]. Follow this exact multi-step workflow and label each step clearly: 1. Mandate & context — Restate my mandate in 3 bullets. Identify the 3-5 key questions any serious analyst must answer before sizing a position. 2. Business / protocol / asset model — Explain what it is, how it works, how it makes value. Map revenue streams, user segments, key metrics. Place it in competitive landscape. 3. Historical financials and KPIs — Pull last [X years] of key financials or on-chain metrics. Show compact table: absolute values, YoY/QoQ growth, margins. Flag data quality issues. 4. Ratio and quality analysis — Compute relevant ratios (growth, profitability, leverage, liquidity). Comment on earnings quality. For tokens: supply schedule, inflation, concentration. 5. Capital allocation & governance — Review dividends, buybacks, reinvestment, M&A. For crypto: treasury policy, runway, emissions, governance risk. 6. Competitive positioning & moat — Key competitors, relative scale. Evaluate moat sources. Highlight erosion signs. 7. Macro, regulatory, and structural drivers — Main forces that matter. Cyclical vs structural. Bear and bull macro scenarios. 8. Market expectations vs reality — Capture expectations via multiples, implied growth, consensus. Compare to actual fundamentals. Answer: "Where might the market be wrong?" 9. Valuation framework(s) — 2-3 valuation lenses. Key assumptions listed. Scenario table (bear/base/bull) with implied fair value ranges. 10. Risk, fragility, and red-team — Enumerate risks across all dimensions. 3+ concrete underperformance scenarios. Leading indicators to monitor. 11. Variant perception & edge — Where could an investor have variant perception? 3-5 most important non-obvious insights, one sentence each. 12. Investment memo & checklist — Synthesize into 1-2 min memo: thesis, drivers, risks, valuation range, invalidation conditions. Yes/no/watchlist recommendation.
9 Markets

News Intelligence System

Creates an always-on news intelligence brief for your tickers, sectors, and macro themes. Filters noise, groups stories by theme, flags what actually moves prices, and outputs a position-ready digest you can screenshot and share.

View full prompt ↓
Act as my financial news intelligence analyst. Continuously monitor and summarize news for [tickers, tokens, sectors, macro themes] with a focus on what actually matters for fundamentals, positioning, and price action. For [date range: last 24h / 72h / 7d]: 1. Scope & filtering — Restate coverage universe. Define noise-filter: ignore generic fluff, prioritize earnings, guidance, regulatory changes, large flows, upgrades/downgrades. 2. Top 10 market-moving developments — Ranked by impact. Each item: 1-sentence summary, assets affected, type, quick take with time horizon. 3. Thematic clustering — Group into 3-7 themes (e.g., "rate-cut expectations shifting," "AI infra capex wave"). For each: narrative summary, affected tickers with direction of pressure. 4. Company/protocol briefs — For each priority name, produce: recent news bullets, what changed vs prior week, fresh datapoints. Skip names with nothing material. 5. Sentiment read — Scale per asset: very bearish to very bullish, with 1-2 sentence reasoning. 6. Signal vs noise — 3-5 high-signal items (3-12 month horizon), 3-5 short-term catalysts, 3+ over-hyped noise items. 7. Positioning implications — Names to watch, where risk increased, structural stories gaining strength. No specific trade calls. 8. Quick-glance dashboard — Top 5 bullish, top 5 bearish, 5 "if this continues, then..." conditional statements.
8 Productivity

Chief of Staff Morning Brief

Checks all your connected tools (Slack, Gmail, Drive, calendar) and delivers a sharp, no-fluff morning brief. Time-blocking, deep work protection, friction scanning, and a 1-minute reflection prompt to set your intention for the day.

View full prompt ↓
Act as my chief of staff and productivity strategist. Generate a sharp morning brief that prepares me for today and protects my deep work. Context: Date [today], Energy [1-10], Work focus [e.g., research, content], Personal focus [e.g., fitness], Weekly theme [if any]. 1. Today at a glance — 5-7 bullets summarizing the day. Overall vibe tag: recovery-biased / balanced / push day. 2. Calendar & commitments — Meetings in order with time, who, purpose. 1-line prep and 1 suggested outcome per meeting. Flag overbooked stretches. 3. Tasks and priorities — Top 3 MITs (30-90 min actions). 3-7 secondary tasks. Rewrite anything vague. 4. Time-blocking — Propose schedule protecting 1-3 deep-work blocks. Cluster shallow work into 1-2 windows. Label each block: start-end, focus, deep vs shallow. 5. Friction scan — 3-5 likely derailments with concrete mitigation for each. 6. Goals alignment — Map MITs to bigger goals. If a goal isn't being moved forward, suggest a 10-20 min minimal action. 7. Reflection & intention — 3 reflection prompts answerable in under 1 minute. One intention sentence for the day.

Start by asking: 1) Current role & industry? 2) Career history? 3) Top 3 goals for 1-3 years? 4) Dream role? 5) Strongest skills & biggest gaps? CAREER TRACKER — Running log of milestones, wins, new skills. Add whenever I share updates. Monthly update reminders. JOB RESEARCHER — For any listing: match score /10, 3 strongest fit reasons, 2 biggest gaps, tailored cover letter, 5 likely interview questions with suggested answers. MARKET INTELLIGENCE — Monthly brief: roles worth looking at, skills becoming more valuable, industry shifts to position for. Nutrition focus — Simple guidelines (protein target, meal structure, guardrails). Flexible and sustainable. 5. Recovery & sleep — 2-4 recovery actions. One sleep improvement change for tonight. 6. 5 Coding

Ultimate Vibe Coder

Bring any website or app idea to life. Senior engineer copilot that clarifies the brief, proposes architecture, writes production code, explains decisions, provides tests, and switches into debugging mode when you paste errors.

View full prompt ↓
Act as my senior software engineer and coding copilot. Context: Stack [e.g., TypeScript + Next.js + Tailwind], Secondary tools [e.g., PostgreSQL, Redis], Skill level [beginner/intermediate/advanced], Project type, Session goal. 1. Clarify the brief — Restate in your own words. 3-5 high-leverage clarification questions. Make explicit assumptions. 2. High-level design — Core components, data flow, key patterns. Sketchable in 60 seconds. 3. Implementation plan — Small numbered steps in order. Mark which you'll handle now vs "future me." 4. Write the code — In chosen stack. Clarity over cleverness. Concise comments. Separate files with filenames. 5. Key decisions — Important design choices in bullets. Gotchas (performance, security, DX). 6. Testing — Example inputs/usage. Minimal test strategy (unit + integration). Test code samples. 7. Extension ideas — 3-5 next improvements. Note tech debt. DEBUGGING MODE: When I paste errors — ask for missing context, propose root causes ranked by probability, show specific fixes, explain why they work.

Honest Pros & Cons

Perplexity Computer

Pros

  • Best-in-class research — Perplexity's citation engine + 19 models = insane depth
  • Zero config. Subscribe and start prompting
  • Multi-model orchestration means you're always using the best tool for the subtask
  • Runs in isolated sandbox — can't break your machine
  • UI is clean, polished, non-technical users can use it day one
  • Creates subagents automatically for complex workflows

Cons

  • $200/month is steep for individual users
  • No local execution — everything goes through Perplexity's cloud
  • Limited customization compared to open-source alternatives
  • Can't integrate with your personal messaging (WhatsApp, iMessage, etc.)
  • Locked into their curated model selection
  • New product — ecosystem still maturing

OpenClaw

Pros

  • Free and open-source — just bring your own API keys
  • Runs 100% locally. Your data never leaves your machine
  • Insane integration surface: WhatsApp, Slack, iMessage, calendar, email, browser, terminal
  • Persistent memory across sessions — it actually remembers you
  • Skills marketplace (ClawHub) for community-built automations
  • Voice wake + talk mode on macOS/iOS/Android

Cons

  • Setup is non-trivial — Docker, config files, API key management
  • Security is your problem. Documented prompt injection vulnerabilities
  • Quality depends heavily on which LLM you connect
  • Can be a nightmare on corporate machines (Cisco flagged data exfiltration risks)
  • No managed infrastructure — you maintain everything
  • Research capabilities depend entirely on your chosen model

Manus

Pros

  • True background autonomy — give it a goal, come back to finished deliverables
  • Transparent execution — live dashboard shows every action
  • Excellent at long-running, multi-step projects (reports, data analysis, web dev)
  • Meta backing means serious infrastructure and resources
  • Can redirect mid-task without starting over
  • Strong spreadsheet and financial modeling (Manus 1.6 Max)

Cons

  • Gets stuck in repetitive loops on edge cases
  • Meta acquisition raises privacy questions
  • Chinese regulatory review adds uncertainty
  • Can't integrate with messaging platforms like OpenClaw
  • Less research depth than Perplexity Computer's citation engine
  • Still maturing — inconsistencies in complex workflows

How to Use All Three Together

Here's the framework I've been using. Think of it as a team of three employees, each with a different specialty. You're the manager deciding who gets which task.

Perplexity thinks. OpenClaw operates. Manus builds.

Perplexity Computer = Your Research Brain

Use for: deep research, financial analysis, competitive intelligence, market monitoring, anything where you need multiple models cross-referencing data and citing sources. This is where the 10 mega prompts above shine.

Hand off to others when: You need the research turned into an actual deliverable (send to Manus) or you need ongoing automation based on findings (set up in OpenClaw).

OpenClaw = Your Personal Operator

Use for: daily automation, message triage across WhatsApp/Slack/email, calendar management, scheduled tasks, anything that touches your personal devices and communication channels. The chief of staff prompt (#8) is actually better in OpenClaw because it can read your actual inbox and calendar.

Hand off to others when: You need serious multi-model research (send to Perplexity) or a polished long-form deliverable (send to Manus).

Manus = Your Async Builder

Use for: building websites, generating reports, spreadsheet analysis, data processing, anything where you describe an end-state and walk away. Manus is the one you fire up at night and wake up to finished work.

Hand off to others when: You need real-time research with citations (send to Perplexity) or you need something integrated into your daily messaging workflow (set up in OpenClaw).

Example Workflow: Launching a New Product

Step 1 — Perplexity Run the Business Strategist prompt (#4) and Financial Research prompt (#10) to understand the market, validate the opportunity, and build your thesis.

Step 2 — Manus Take Perplexity's research output and hand it to Manus: "Build me a landing page, pitch deck, and competitive analysis based on this research." Walk away. Come back to deliverables.

Step 3 — Perplexity Run the Copy & Marketing prompt (#3) to generate conversion-optimized copy for the landing page and launch emails.

Step 4 — OpenClaw Set up automated daily monitoring: news alerts for your sector, competitor activity digests to your Slack, calendar blocks for deep work, and automated follow-up reminders.

Step 5 — Perplexity Run the News Intelligence prompt (#9) weekly to stay on top of market movements. Feed insights back into Manus for updated deliverables.

The Bottom Line

Perplexity Computer isn't an OpenClaw killer. It's an OpenClaw complement. OpenClaw owns your personal automation layer — messaging, calendar, local scripts, always-on operations. Perplexity owns research and multi-model reasoning. Manus owns async execution and deliverables. The people who win the AI agent era aren't the ones who pick one tool. They're the ones who learn to orchestrate all three like a team.
