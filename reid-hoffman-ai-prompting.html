How Reid Hoffman Actually Uses AI — and What You Can Steal

The LinkedIn founder's prompting playbook, broken down into three tiers anyone can learn. No coding required.

Julie Zhang · Feb 2026 · 4 min read

Reid Hoffman shares a framework for how to actually get good at using AI. He breaks it into three tiers — basic, medium, and advanced. Here's the breakdown for non-technical people.

Basic

Make AI Pull Information — Don't Let It Guess

When you ask AI a question, it auto-generates an answer based on training data that could be 18 months old. Hoffman's most basic technique: prompt it to actually pull and search for current information instead of guessing from stale data. Tell it to search, cite sources, check recent data. That one shift makes everything more reliable.

Medium

Talk to All the Agents — Compare What Works

Don't use one AI. Talk to all the agents — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, whatever — then compare: what can we derive from what's working and what's not? Where do they agree? Where do they diverge? The disagreements are where the real insights hide.

Advanced

Five Techniques That Change Everything

1

Ask: What Additional Data Would Be Useful?

Don't just ask AI for an answer. Ask it: "What pulls from additional data that might be useful?" Let it suggest data sources you haven't thought of. Your data should include both internal data (your own notes, docs, past work) and external data (news, reports, public information). Feed both in.

2

Compute Is an Investment

Think of it as a vector where AI is useful: spending $10K on compute could give you 15 different useful things that are each easily valuable on their own. A market analysis, a competitive brief, a strategy memo, a risk assessment. The ROI per dollar of compute is the metric that matters.

3

Lead the AI, Then Let It Write Your Prompt

First, lead the AI agent by chatting about the research for a bit — give it context, tell it what you're exploring. Then, ask it to write you the right prompt to do all the right research. It gives you a 2-page prompt. Then you run that 2-page prompt. The result is dramatically better than anything you'd have typed yourself.

4

Role-Based Perspective Prompting

Ask AI: "What would a technologist say? What would a VC say? What would a government policy person say? What would a nuclear safety person say? Are there other roles I haven't thought of?" Then ask it to adopt each role and give you the answers from that perspective. You get a full 360-degree view of any problem without hiring a single consultant.

The Takeaway

Basic = make AI search instead of guess. Medium = talk to all the agents and compare what works. Advanced = ask what data you're missing, treat compute as investment, let AI write your prompts, and run every question through multiple expert perspectives.
