002 / AI Works

Things I made with AI

🧸 GPT-4o · 2025
Product Design Image Gen

AI Plushie Collection

Designed original plushie characters using OpenAI 4o image generation. Published on RedNote where they went viral with the Chinese creator community.

Process & Technique

Started with text descriptions of character personalities, iterated with 4o's image gen to nail the form factor, then refined color palettes through 20+ prompt variations. Key insight: describing the plushie's "emotional vibe" yielded better results than describing physical features.

🎬 Sora / Runway · 2025-26
AI Video Content

AI Video Portfolio

Produced short-form AI videos across multiple platforms using Sora, Runway Gen-3, and Kling. Content ranges from aesthetic mood pieces to product showcases.

Process & Technique

Each tool has a personality. Sora excels at cinematic motion, Runway at style consistency, Kling at speed. I storyboard first in text, generate 3-5 variations per scene, then edit in CapCut/Premiere. The human eye for pacing and emotion is what turns AI clips into actual content.

🖼️ Midjourney / 4o · 2024-26
AI Image Visual Art

AI Image Generation Work

Ongoing body of AI-generated visual work spanning product mockups, editorial imagery, character design, and abstract art. Published across Instagram, RedNote, and X.

Process & Technique

I treat prompting as a creative discipline, not just typing. Reference images + style keywords + negative prompts + seed iteration. Midjourney for aesthetics, DALL-E/4o for accuracy, Stable Diffusion for control. Always hand-curate — AI generates, human taste selects.

Code Web Dev

AI-Assisted Coding Projects

Built websites, tools, and prototypes using AI coding assistants. This portfolio site, the NeurIPS workshop website, and Solartopia projects were all built with AI as co-pilot.

003 / How I Work

My AI process

01

Vision First, Tools Second

I start with what I want the final thing to feel like — not which AI tool to use. The emotional intent drives the prompt. Tool selection comes after the vision is clear.

02

Iterate Relentlessly

First output is never the final output. I generate 20-50 variations, curate the best 3, then refine. AI gives you volume. Human taste gives you quality.

03

Cross-Pollinate Tools

One project, multiple AIs. Image gen for mood boards, video gen for motion tests, code gen for prototypes, language models for copy. Each tool has a strength. Use all of them.

04

Document Everything

Every prompt, every iteration, every decision. The process is the content. Showing how you made something is often more valuable than the thing itself.

05

Ship Fast, Reflect Slow

AI lets you produce at 10x speed. But the thinking underneath should be deep. Publish quickly, then write about what you learned. Speed of output, depth of thought.

06

Taste Is the Moat

Everyone has access to the same AI tools. The differentiator is knowing what's good. Taste, curation, and editorial judgment are the skills AI can't replace.
